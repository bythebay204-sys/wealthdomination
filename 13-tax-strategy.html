Module 08 — The Billionaire Blueprint

Tax Strategy & Elimination

Taxes are the single largest expense in most people's financial lives — and the most negotiable. The tax code was not written for W-2 employees. It was written for business owners, investors, and real estate operators. This final module shows you how to use it the way it was actually designed to be used.

The 4 Tax Elimination Strategies

01

Entity Election

An LLC taxed as an S-Corp can save $10,000-$30,000 per year for a self-employed person earning $100K+. The election is a form filed with the IRS. Most people never file it. Their accountants never mention it.

02

Depreciation & Cost Segregation

Real property can be depreciated over 27.5 or 39 years. Cost segregation accelerates that depreciation to 5-7 years, creating massive paper losses that offset real income. Legal, IRS-sanctioned, and wildly underused.

03

The Augusta Rule

IRS Section 280A allows you to rent your home to your business for up to 14 days per year, tax-free to you personally. On meetings, strategy sessions, or board meetings at your residence. The business deducts it. You receive it tax-free.

04

Qualified Business Income Deduction

The QBI deduction allows pass-through business owners to deduct up to 20% of qualified business income from their taxable income. Combined with S-Corp election and retirement contributions, most self-employed people can cut their effective tax rate to under 15%.

The Full Tax Elimination Stack

Retirement Accounts as Tax Shelters

A Solo 401k allows contributions up to $66,000 per year (2024). A SEP-IRA allows 25% of net self-employment income. These contributions are deducted before taxes are calculated. At a 30% effective rate, $66,000 in contributions saves $19,800 in taxes — every year.

Real Estate as a Tax Engine

Rental income sheltered by depreciation. Interest deductions. Property tax deductions. Travel deductions for property visits. A real estate professional designation eliminates the passive activity loss limitations entirely. Real estate may be the most tax-efficient asset class available.

The Business Deduction Stack

Home office. Vehicle use. Business meals (50%). Health insurance premiums. Education and training. Travel for business purposes. Software, tools, and subscriptions. Professional services. Every legitimate business expense reduces your taxable income dollar for dollar.

Know When to Hire a CPA

A competent CPA who works with business owners and investors is not an expense — it is a multiplier. The wrong CPA files your taxes. The right CPA designs your tax strategy before the year ends. The difference is often $20,000-$50,000 per year in legal tax savings.

Cut Your Tax Bill This Year

Three moves before December 31st.

  1. Ask your CPA or accountant: 'Should my LLC be taxed as an S-Corp?' If they don't know, find a new CPA.
  2. List every expense you paid personally this year that was for business purposes. These are deductions you missed.
  3. Open and fund a Solo 401k or SEP-IRA before your tax filing deadline. Every dollar contributed is a dollar not taxed.
